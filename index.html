Frequently Asked Questions

NFT stands for "non-fungible token." It's a digital certificate of ownership that's stored on a blockchain, a decentralized digital ledger used to record transactions. Unlike cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin or Ethereum, which are fungible and can be exchanged on a one-to-one basis, NFTs are unique and not mutually interchangeable. Each NFT contains distinctive information that distinguishes it from other tokens, making it one-of-a-kind and irreplaceable. These tokens are often used to re present ownership or proof of authenticity of digital assets like art, videos, music, collectibles, and more..

Choose a Platform: There are various marketplaces specialized in selling NFTs, like OpenSea, Rarible, and others. Select a platform that suits your preferences and the type of NFTs you're interested in. Set Up a Wallet: You'll need a cryptocurrency wallet that's compatible with the platform you choose. Most often, you'll use Ethereum-based tokens for NFT transactions. Consider wallets like MetaMask or Trust Wallet. Buy Ethereum (ETH): Since many NFTs are bought using Ethereum, you'll need to purchase ETH from a cryptocurrency exchange. You can then transfer this ETH to your crypto wallet. Store Your NFTs: After purchase, your NFTs will be stored in your digital wallet. It's crucial to secure your wallet, as losing access means losing your NFTs. Consider the Risks: As with any investment, there are risks involved. NFT values can be highly volatile and speculative. It's essential to research and understand the market before investing.

The security of an NFT primarily depends on the blockchain it's built on and the platform where it's bought, sold, or stored. Blockchains like Ethereum, which most NFTs are based on, are considered secure due to their decentralized nature and cryptographic protocols. However, vulnerabilities can exist in the smart contracts or the platforms themselves.
